I am a justice-oriented worker dedicated to education, cultural heritage, and public knowledge. 

I have spent most of my career working in the humanities, which encompasses the study of art, history, language, and culture. I have written about labor in higher education, about the use of technology in accessing the past and reshaping the future, and about the importance of radical transparency, shared vulnerability, and collective action in higher education.

Since 2011, I have held positions in universities, academic libraries, and the civil service. In 2025, I co-founded [Sidra Collaborative](https://sidracollaborative.com/), a consultancy that partners with funders and organizations in the arts, culture, and humanities to increase transparency, equity, and impact in grantmaking and philanthropy. From 2019-2025, I worked at the National Endowment for the Humanities, where I helped redirect taxpayer dollars towards the public good through grants and fellowships related to digital humanities, open access publication, and understanding the state of the humanities. I previously worked at Brown University and the University of Texas at Austin, where I earned my PhD in 2017. [Learn more about my academic research here.](/categories/research/)

I have written and spoken extensively about labor and careers in the humanities. In 2026, I was elected to the Executive Board of the Association for Computers in the Humanities. From 2021-2025, I was the acting unit vice president of NEH AFGE Local 3403, and in 2020, I co-founded the [Visionary Futures Collective](https://visionary-futures-collective.github.io/), a loosely organized group of humanities professionals dedicated to labor issues during the first years of the coronavirus pandemic. [Learn more about my advocacy work here.](/categories/advocacy/)

View my [cv](/pdf/halperta_cv.pdf) or [resume](/pdf/halperta_resume.pdf).

# On Technology

My work depends on technology. 

I use [Scrivener](https://scrivener.software/) for longform composition, [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md/) for records management, [Sublime Text](https://www.sublimetext.com/) for text editing, [Acorn](https://flyingmeat.com/acorn/) for image editing, and Google for email, spreadsheets, and collaborative writing. My website is built and maintained with [Jekyll](https://jekyllrb.com/) and [So Simple](https://github.com/mmistakes/so-simple-theme), and hosted on GitHub. I work on a MacBook Pro and make prints using a Risograph GR 3700. Some of my early scholarship experimented with Ocular, a machine learning tool for text and image analysis built with generative models that had some similarities to what we now call generative AI. 

It is very difficult to be intentional and values-aligned when working with technology, and I continue changing my approach even as the landscape changes around us.

I do not and have never used corporate generative AI tools like Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or CoPilot in my work. I do not use AI integration for word processing, internet searching, web design, or image or audio editing. I do not use GenAI tools to manage projects, conduct research, organize or analyze data, produce or revise writing, or generate outputs.
